Design, Integration, and Evaluation of a Dual-Arm Robotic System for High-Throughput Tissue Sampling from Potato Tubers

Divyanth Loganathan Girija, Manoj Karkee
Paper Video Code Data

🔍 Overview

Manual tissue sampling for molecular-based pathogen detection in potato tubers remains a bottleneck due to its labor-intensive and physically demanding nature. To address this challenge, this study developed a dual-arm coordinated inline robotic system that integrates tuber grasping and tissue sampling mechanisms with a machine vision-guided control scheme to automate tissue sampling from potato tubers.

Functionally, tubers get transported onto a conveyor system that halts when a YOLO11-based vision system identified a tuber entering the workspace of a one-prismatic (P)-degree-of-freedom (DoF) tuber grasping arm, equipped with a gripping end-effector. This arm securely grasped the tuber and positioned it for tissue sampling using the second robotic arm, a 3-P-DoF Cartesian manipulator with a biopsy punch-based end-effector. Using this system, tissue sampling was performed at a specific location on the tubers (eyes or stolon scars), detected and localized by YOLOv10-based vision system. The tissue-sampling operation followed a four-stage process: insertion of the end-effector into the tuber, rotation of the biopsy punch for tissue detachment from the tuber, biopsy punch extraction, and deposition of the tissue core onto designated collection location.

Results showed an average positional error of 1.84 mm in reaching the sampling location along the tuber surface, and an average depth deviation of 1.79 mm from the intended 7.00 mm punching depth. The overall success rate for tissue core extraction and deposition onto the designated collection location was 81.5%, with an average sampling cycle time of 10.4 s. 📂 Dataset

We are releasing a dataset of 900 potato tuber images, encompassing multiple cultivars, including Umatilla Russet, Russet Burbank, and Ute Russet (russet-skinned), Ciklamen (red-skinned), and Purple Pelisse (purple-skinned). This dataset is designed for developing deep-learning models to detect eyes and stolon scars.
